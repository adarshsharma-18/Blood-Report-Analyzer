Understanding Blood Reports

Blood tests and reports help provide a snapshot of your overall health by analyzing different components of your blood, allowing doctors to detect potential issues like infections, anemia, bleeding disorders, or problems with your immune system, and to monitor chronic conditions or the effectiveness of treatments.


What general blood reports include

Red blood cell count (RBC): Indicates the number of oxygen-carrying red blood cells.
  • White blood cell count (WBC): Shows the number of white blood cells involved in fighting infections.
  • Platelet count: Measures the number of platelets responsible for blood clotting.
  • Hemoglobin level: The amount of protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen.
  • Hematocrit level: The percentage of red blood cells in the blood.
